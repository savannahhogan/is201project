The Boy Next Door - Meg Cabot

A BOOK REVIEW

Posted by Savannah Hogan on April 13, 2020

The first thing you need to know about The Boy Next Door is that it is composed of emails. Literally the entire book is emails. Emails from Mel (our protagonist) emails to Mel, emails about Mel. The entire narrative is made up of emails.

Is this a bad thing? In this case, kind of. I have read other books written from the perspective of letters/texts/emails, and I have to say that this one doesn't do it very well. (If you're looking for a book that's really a collection of communication I'd suggest both Love, Rosie and We Are Still Tornados before The Boy Next Door.)

The Boy Next Door tells us the story of Mel, a gossip columinst in NYC who'd really like to branch into "real" journalism (doesn't this sound like every romcom you've ever seen?). I didn't really find myself rooting for the characters, and most of the humor fell flat. Oh, also she ends up falling in love. But that too, didn't really do it for me.

While I didn't love this book at all, I DID finish it, which is something I can't always say. Is it worth the read? If you're bored, yeah, it's worth the read. It doesn't take very long because its just emails. But seriously, you won't get too much out of it. I have a hard time even classifying it as "brain candy" because I just didn't enjoy it that much.
